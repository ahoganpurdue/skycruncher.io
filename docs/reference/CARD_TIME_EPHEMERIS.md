# Time & Ephemeris — algorithm card (clock, sidereal, Kepler lane)

Symbols: JD=Julian Date; d=days since J2000 (JD−2451545.0); T=Julian centuries ((JD−2451545)/36525); (α,δ)=RA/Dec, **RA in HOURS** unless stated; GMST/LST unit (hours vs degrees) stated per entry — this lane runs BOTH; M/E=mean/eccentric anomaly, **degrees** (Schlyter-style, not radians); ε=obliquity; π=horizontal parallax.
Every entry below is VERIFIED against the code at the cited line; none are assumed from the paper alone.

### 1. Unix→Julian Date conversion + Julian centuries/years since J2000
- **Formula**: JD = Date.getTime()/86400000 + 2440587.5 (Unix epoch 1970-01-01T00:00Z = JD 2440587.5). T = (JD − 2451545.0)/36525; years = (JD − J2000)/365.25; d = JD − J2000.
- **Code**: `src/engine/core/TimeService.ts:30-33` (`toJulianDate`), `:38-40` (`toJuliancenturies`), `:45-47` (`toJulianYears`), `:52-55` (`toDayssinceJ2000`); J2000=2451545.0 at `:18`. Inverse (JD→Date) lives separately in `src/engine/pipeline/m1_ingestion/fits_decoder.ts:357` (`(jd − 2440587.5)·86400000`).
- **Cite**: standard identity; Meeus, *Astronomical Algorithms*, ch. 7 form [VERIFY — reference book, not peer-reviewed; the Unix-ms shortcut is a programming identity, not from the literature].
- **Gotcha**: the whole lane runs on **UTC treated as UT1≈TT** — no ΔT, no leap seconds, no TDB anywhere (fine at the arcminute ephemeris grade shipped). String inputs: `new Date(string)` parses a bare ISO date-time as **LOCAL** time in JS — the FITS decoder force-appends `'Z'` upstream (`fits_decoder.ts:342-346`) precisely so this method never sees a naked string; EXIF path normalizes `YYYY:MM:DD` → `YYYY-MM-DD` first (`metadata_reaper.ts:204-212`).

### 2. GMST — IAU-1982 lineage, THREE live numeric forms
- **Form A (degrees, continuous, full)**: GMST° = 280.46061837 + 360.98564736629·(JD−2451545.0) + 0.000387933·T² − T³/38710000, normalized `((g%360)+360)%360` — `src/engine/core/TimeService.ts:62-68` (`getGMST_Deg`); LMST° = (GMST + lon%360 + 360)%360 at `:70-72`.
- **Form B (degrees, continuous, TRUNCATED — no T² terms)**: GMST° = (280.46061837 + 360.98564736629·D) % 360 — `src/engine/pipeline/m10_psf/psf_physics.ts:148-151` (`targetGeometry`, feeds the M10 airmass/hour-angle report) and `tools/adaptive/image_conditions.ts:71`.
- **Form C (hours, linear)**: GMST_h = (18.697374558 + 24.06570982441908·d) % 24 — `src/engine/wasm_compute/src/ephemeris.rs:226` (alt/az) and `:267` (topocentric), `src/engine/wasm_compute/src/bright_star_atlas.rs:29` (`get_lst`), `src/engine/pipeline/m6_plate_solve/planetary_adapter.ts:57`; code-generated again by `tools/format_atlas_rust.ts:27`.
- **Consistency**: A, B, C share one lineage — 18.697374558 h × 15 = 280.46061837° exactly, and 24.06570982441908 h/day × 15 = 360.98564736629 °/day to 12 significant figures. B and C ≡ A minus the T² terms (≈0.1″ at T≈0.26, i.e. 2026 — negligible for these consumers).
- **Cite**: Aoki, Guinot, Kaplan, Kinoshita, McCarthy & Seidelmann (1982), "The new definition of universal time", A&A 105, 359 (IAU-1982 GMST). The hours-linear constants are the USNO "approximate sidereal time" reduction [VERIFY — almanac/tutorial derivation, not a separate peer-reviewed source].
- **Gotcha**: no equation of the equinoxes anywhere — GMST is used where GAST is formally required (≈±16″ ≈ 1 s of time, ignored). `planetary_adapter.ts:57` does NOT reduce gst before use (`:58` reduces lst % 24 and skips the +24 clamp) — for pre-J2000 dates lst goes negative; harmless there because the offset is exactly −24 h (−360°) and the only consumers are 360°-periodic trig (`:61-63`), but copying that LST into any NON-periodic use (clock display, differencing against another clock, a time inversion like entry 8) would break. The house trap is the UNIT, not the epoch: Form A/B return DEGREES, Form C returns HOURS.

### 3. Precession J2000↔date (Lieske ζ/z/θ) + proper motion (pmra·cosδ) + Alt/Az ⇄ RA/Dec
- **Precession (J2000→date)**: ζ = (2306.2181·T + 0.30188·T² + 0.017998·T³)/3600 °, z = (2306.2181·T + 1.09468·T² + 0.018203·T³)/3600 °, θ = (2004.3109·T − 0.42665·T² − 0.041833·T³)/3600 °; A = cosδ·sin(α+ζ), B = cosθ·cosδ·cos(α+ζ) − sinθ·sinδ, C = sinθ·cosδ·cos(α+ζ) + cosθ·sinδ; α′ = atan2(A,B) + z (returned in HOURS, wrapped +24 mod 24), δ′ = asin(clamp(C)). `src/engine/core/TimeService.ts:80-110`.
- **Proper motion**: pmra is Gaia-convention μα* = μα·cosδ in mas/yr; Δα° = (pmra/3.6e6)·years / cosδ (guard |cosδ|>1e-6 else 0), applied in HOURS (/15); Δδ° = (pmdec/3.6e6)·years, dec clamped ±90. `:130-148`. Live path: `m5_coordinate_flatten/zenith.ts:87-89` applies PM first, THEN precesses.
- **Alt/Az**: HA = LMST − α; sinAlt = sinφ·sinδ + cosφ·cosδ·cosH; az = acos((sinδ − sinφ·sinAlt)/(cosφ·cosAlt)), mirrored az → 2π−az when sin(HA)>0 ⇒ azimuth from NORTH through EAST. `:155-180` (+ LMST-injected variant `:182-197`). Inverse (`horizontalToEquatorial`, `:202-231`): δ = asin(sinφ·sinh + cosφ·cosh·cosA); α = LST ∓ HA selected by sign of sin(A); RA returned in HOURS.
- **Cite**: Lieske, Lederle, Fricke & Morando (1977), A&A 58, 1 + Lieske (1979), A&A 73, 282 (IAU-1976 precession angles — coefficients match 2306.2181/2004.3109 exactly). PM convention: Lindegren et al. (2021), A&A 649, A2 (Gaia EDR3 astrometry, μα* ≡ μα·cosδ).
- **Gotcha**: the docstring at `:77` claims "(IAU 2006)" — FALSE; the live coefficients are IAU-1976/Lieske (IAU-2006 would be 2306.083227″… per Capitaine et al. 2003, A&A 412, 567). Stale comment, not a math bug (≲0.1″/century difference). `precessDateToJ2000` (`:115-123`) is NOT a true inverse — it mirrors the JD about J2000 (jdInv = J2000 + (J2000−jd), i.e. T→−T in the FORWARD polynomials) instead of transposing the rotation; second-order error only, and it currently has NO production caller (grep-verified; only `precessJ2000ToDate` is wrapped by `zenith.ts:70-75`). Azimuth acos form divides by cos(alt) — singular at the zenith. No refraction anywhere in this chain (deferred per CLAUDE.md).

### 4. Kepler solve (5 fixed Newton steps) + Schlyter elements + magBase law + hierarchical assembly + fixed obliquity
- **Kepler (all in DEGREES)**: n = 0.9856076686/a^1.5 °/day (`ephemeris.rs:82`); M = (m + n·d) mod 360 (`:83-84`); seed E₀ = M + e·(180/π)·sin M·(1 + e·cos M) (`:86`); then EXACTLY 5 Newton steps E ← E − (E − e·(180/π)·sin E − M)/(1 − e·cos E) (`:88-91`) — **no convergence check, no high-e guard** (max e in the DB is Pluto 0.24880766, so 5 steps converge to sub-arcsec). Position: xv = a(cos E − e), yv = a√(1−e²)·sin E, ν = atan2(yv,xv), r = √(xv²+yv²), rotated by (ω, Ω=N, i) to a heliocentric ECLIPTIC vector (`:93-108`).
- **Assembly**: `batch_solve_ephemeris` (`src/engine/wasm_compute/src/ephemeris.rs:117-256`) — Sun pinned at origin (`:129`), per-body local vectors, recursive parent-vector addition `resolve_absolute` (depth cap 10, unknown parent → zero vector, `:139-170`), geocentric = absolute − Earth (`:186-191`).
- **Magnitude**: mag = mag_base + 5·log₁₀(r_helio·dist_au) (`:200`), Sun hardcoded −26.7 (`:197-198`); skip non-Luna moons when mag>20 (`:204`). **No phase-angle term at all.**
- **Ecliptic→equatorial**: FIXED obliquity ε = 23.43929° (`:179`): y_eq = y·cosε − z·sinε, z_eq = y·sinε + z·cosε (`:207-209`); RA converted to HOURS (`:214-215`). Element tables: `src/engine/core/celestial_data.ts:38-97` (Sun→Luna; e.g. Neptune M=260.2471, Moon M=115.3654 — Schlyter's table values verbatim), minor moons `:100-219`.
- **Cite**: element sets & degree-space Newton iteration match Paul Schlyter, "Computing planetary positions" (stjarnhimlen.se/comp/ppcomp.html) [VERIFY — tutorial source, not peer-reviewed]. The distance-modulus magnitude convention (V(1,0) + 5·log₁₀(rΔ) + phase) is the Astronomical Almanac system — Mallama & Hilton (2018), Astronomy & Computing 25, 10.
- **Gotcha (mixed-convention magBase — FIXED 2026-07-09)**: the law is only valid with absolute V(1,0)-style constants; the DB originally mixed conventions (Jupiter −2.70, Saturn 0.46, Uranus 5.52, Neptune 7.84, Pluto 14.3, Luna −12.74 were mean APPARENT magnitudes — Jupiter at opposition computed ≈ +4.0 vs true ≈ −2.7; the Moon ≈ −25.7). All entries are now V(1,0): Jupiter −9.40, Saturn −8.88 (globe only, no rings), Uranus −7.19, Neptune −6.87, Pluto −0.45 (H_V), Luna +0.21 (law then yields the FULL-MOON-equivalent value ≈ −12.7 — phase still not modeled); satellite rows derived via H = m_opp − 5·log₁₀(a_parent·(a_parent−1)), which reproduces published H (Phobos 11.8, Titan −1.3, Janus 4.9, …). Consequence of the fix: honest satellite mags are all <20, so the `mag>20` moon skip (`:204`) no longer prunes them — all bodies now appear in results. Regression anchor: `src/engine/tests/ephemeris.test.ts` pins Jupiter ≈ −2.9 (2023-11-03 opposition) and Saturn ≈ +0.5 (2023-08-27) through the REAL compiled WASM. `mag` remains display/routing-grade only (no phase term); do not use it photometrically. Also: `batch_solve_ephemeris` takes a `_jd` argument it IGNORES (`:122`) — d is the only clock. Shipped-binary caveat: this fn runs from the compiled `pkg/` WASM; the 2026-06-16 stale-binary incident means any edit here is invisible until a `wasm-pack` rebuild (N/A for this fix — magBase flows in from TS at call time).

### 5. Epoch-adjusted mean anomaly pre-compensation (Schlyter epoch + parent-mass scaling + Moon rate)
- **Problem**: the WASM always derives n from the SUN's GM (entry 4); for satellites that is wrong by √(M_sun/M_parent), and the DB's M values are Schlyter-epoch d₀ = JD 2451543.5 (1999-12-31 00:00 UTC), 1.5 days before J2000.
- **Formula**: M′ = (M + n_true·1.5 + (n_true − n_wasm)·d) mod 360 (clamped ≥0), constructed so M′ + n_wasm·d ≡ M + n_true·(d + 1.5) at the single requested d. n_wasm = 0.9856076686/a^1.5; n_true = n_wasm (heliocentric), 13.0649929509 °/day (Luna — Schlyter's anomalistic Moon rate, ≈ 360°/27.55455 d), n_wasm·√(M_parent/M_sun) otherwise (SUN_MASS_KG = 1.989e30).
- **Code**: `src/engine/core/EphemerisEngine.ts:122-143` (`epochAdjustedMeanAnomaly`; SCHLYTER_EPOCH_OFFSET_DAYS=1.5 at `:127`, n_wasm `:130`, n_true ladder `:134-138`, M′ `:140-142`); injected at `:52` before the WASM batch call `:57`. Rationale comment `:106-121`.
- **Cite**: Kepler's third law n ∝ √(GM_parent)/a^1.5 — textbook two-body mechanics; epoch/rate values from Schlyter [VERIFY — tutorial source].
- **Gotcha**: the compensation is exact only AT the requested d — the adjusted elements are single-instant, not reusable. The +1.5 d epoch shift is 1.5·n of mean anomaly — ~19.6° for the Moon (the original bug) and NOT sub-degree for all planets: ~6.1° Mercury, ~2.4° Venus, ~1.5° Earth, <1° only from Mars outward; the in-code comment's "Harmless for planets (<1 deg)" (`:119`) understates the inner planets (all fully compensated either way, since heliocentric n_true=n_wasm still gets the +1.5·n term). Non-Luna satellites are honestly labeled "decorative markers" in the comment (`:133`) — their parent-mass rate fixes motion, not their crude i/N/w≈0 elements. Results memoized by (date,lat,lon) (`:21-27`). `FAINT_MOON_MAG_CUTOFF=12` at `:11` is a dead constant (never referenced).

### 6. Topocentric parallax correction (Moon / dist < 0.1 AU)
- **Trigger**: body_type=="MOON" OR dist_au < 0.1 (`ephemeris.rs:219-223`).
- **Formula**: π = asin(R_earth/dist_km), R_earth = 6378.14 km, AU = 149597870.7 km (`:263-265`); clock = GMST hours-form (entry 2 Form C) → LST → HA in radians (`:267-271`); A = cosδ·sinH, B = cosδ·cosH − cosφ·sinπ, C = sinδ − sinφ·sinπ (`:278-280`); H′ = atan2(A,B); α′ = LST − H′ (HOURS, wrapped +24); δ′ = atan2(C·cosH′, B) (`:282-286`).
- **Code**: `src/engine/wasm_compute/src/ephemeris.rs:258-289` (`apply_topocentric_correction_internal`), wasm export `:291-299`.
- **Cite**: HA-space parallax reduction after Meeus, *Astronomical Algorithms*, ch. 40 form [VERIFY — book] — but SIMPLIFIED: ρsinφ′/ρcosφ′ are replaced by sinφ/cosφ (`:275-276`).
- **Gotcha**: **spherical Earth, sea-level observer** — no geodetic→geocentric latitude conversion (Meeus's ρ, φ′ with flattening 1/298.257 omitted; worst-case ~0.19° latitude error → arcsec-to-arcmin-scale Moon shift) and no observer elevation. π uses asin (fine). Same shipped-binary caveat as entry 4: the 2026-06-16 stale-binary incident specifically flagged this fn as frozen until rebuild.

### 7. Timestamp trust ladder + stack exposure-midpoint
- **Ladder** (enum `'EXIF' | 'FITS' | 'DEFAULT' | 'USER'`, `src/engine/types/schema.ts:136`): EXIF path prefers DateTimeOriginal > CreateDate > ModifyDate — all stamped `'EXIF'` (`m1_ingestion/metadata_reaper.ts:214-223`); FITS path stamps `'FITS'` (below); `'USER'` only ever set by a manual UI edit (`ui/PipelineWizard.tsx:299`).
- **FITS midpoint** (`m1_ingestion/fits_decoder.ts:331-390`): DATE-OBS forced UTC when no TZ designator (regex `/(?:Z|[+-]\d{2}:?\d{2})$/i`, `:342-346`). If DATE-END exists — or Siril-convention EXPSTART/EXPEND (Julian Dates) with 0 < span ≤ **2 days** (`:365-368`; guard exists because real 446-day multi-month M51 composites were observed, `:354-356`) — timestamp = (start+end)/2, the integration midpoint (`:369-373`). Honest degrade: stacked file (STACKCNT>1) with no end marker keeps DATE-OBS as-is + an explicit "may be offset from the integration midpoint" warning rather than guessing a direction (`:374-380`, rationale `:334-341`). No DATE-OBS at all → EXPSTART JD (sanity > 2400000) with warning (`:385-390`). STACKCNT/NSTACK + LIVETIME/TOTALEXP captured either way (`:349-352`).
- **Consumer gate**: `clockReal = i.timestampTrusted && meta?.timestamp_source && meta.timestamp_source !== 'DEFAULT'` (`pipeline/stages/psf_attribution.ts:349`) gates all observing-geometry outputs; `timestampTrusted` itself comes from unset-clock forensics — year < 2004, > now+2 d, or the Jan-1 00:xx dead-battery signature (`getUTCHours()===0` — the whole midnight hour, not just 00:0x) → untrusted (`pipeline/orchestrator_session.ts:304-327`).
- **Cite**: DATE-OBS-is-UTC and DATE-END semantics: Pence, Chiappetti, Page, Shaw & Stobie (2010), "Definition of the Flexible Image Transport System (FITS), version 3.0", A&A 524, A42. EXPSTART/EXPEND-as-JD is a Siril tool convention [VERIFY — software convention, not a standard].
- **Gotcha**: `'DEFAULT'` IS assigned — it is the initialized baseline of every reaper metadata object (`metadata_reaper.ts:39,144,281`: `createDefaultHard` plus the EXIF and FITS paths' initial `hard`, always paired with `timestamp = new Date().toISOString()`) and the UI fallback (`MainApp.tsx:51`); it survives whenever no EXIF/FITS stamp is found. That makes the enum test load-bearing, not decorative: a defaulted timestamp is wall-clock "now", which PASSES the unset-clock forensics (plausible year, not future) and reaches the gate with `timestampTrusted=true` — the explicit `!== 'DEFAULT'` check at `psf_attribution.ts:349` is the only thing failing it closed. The plate solve is deliberately time-independent; only ephemeris/alt-az/airmass consumers ride this ladder.

### 8. Astrometric time inversion — first-order GMST→JD inverse (forensic timestamp recovery)
- **Formula**: per anchor star, cos H = (sin alt − sinφ·sinδ)/(cosφ·cosδ) (`src-tauri/src/solver/sextant.rs:86`, guards: alt ∈ [1°,85°] `:69`, pole/zenith divisors `:81-83`, |cos H| ≤ 1 `:89`); east/west sign from pixel_x > crpix[0] (`:101-106`); LST_h = (LST°/15 + 24) mod 24 (`:109`); GMST_h = (LST_h − lon/15 + 24) mod 24 (`:112`); then T ≈ (GMST_h − 6.697374558)/2400.051336, JD = 2451545.0 + 36525·T (`:153-156`). Consensus = median of per-anchor JDs (`:122-129`) + std-dev spread (`:131-135`).
- **Code**: `src-tauri/src/solver/sextant.rs:49-156` (`solve_with_dem` + `gmst_to_jd`) — **FILE DELETED @7288cfc; line refs are to the historical blob** (retagged 2026-07-11 per owner-approved D-card-ephemeris-stale-cite). NOTE: this Rust-side lane worked in RA **DEGREES** (`StarAltitudeAnchor.ra_deg`, `:13-15`) — opposite of the TS-side hours convention.
- **Cite**: inverts the 0h-UT GMST seed polynomial GMST = 6.697374558 + 2400.051336·T + 0.000025862·T² (comment `:147-151`) — Meeus, *Astronomical Algorithms*, eq. 12.2 lineage [VERIFY — book], first-order (T² term dropped).
- **Gotcha**: **DELETED @7288cfc — section preserved as the sextant P1 design record, not live code** (historical state at deletion: compiled and unit-tested, no production caller; only `pub mod sextant;` in `solver/mod.rs:2` plus its own `#[cfg(test)]` block). And the inversion as written is **date-ambiguous by construction**: GMST is reduced mod 24 h before inversion, so T ∈ [−0.0028, +0.0072) centuries — every recovered JD lands within ~1 year of J2000 regardless of the true capture epoch. It recovers sidereal PHASE (time of day), not a date; a caller would have to supply the day number externally. The east/west disambiguation via pixel-x vs CRPIX assumes a specific camera orientation/parity — unverified proxy.

## UNIT WARNINGS
- RA is **HOURS** on the TS side everywhere (TimeService args, wasm ephemeris results `ra`, `planetary_adapter` `ra_hours`) — but **DEGREES** on the Rust solver side (`sextant.rs` `ra_deg`) and in FITS headers (`RA` header → `ra_hint` via /15, `fits_decoder.ts:401-403`).
- GMST/LST carry THREE numeric conventions (entry 2): degrees-full (TimeService), degrees-truncated (psf_physics, image_conditions), hours-linear (all WASM + planetary_adapter). Mixing an hours value into a degrees formula is silent — no runtime unit check anywhere.
- Kepler math (entry 4) is in **DEGREES** with explicit 180/π factors inside the Newton step — porting it to a radians library without dropping those factors double-converts.
- Orbital-element epoch: CELESTIAL_DB mean anomalies are **Schlyter d₀ = JD 2451543.5**, NOT J2000 — raw elements must pass through `epochAdjustedMeanAnomaly` (entry 5) before the WASM; feeding them directly loses 1.5 days (≈19.6° for the Moon).
- `batch_solve_ephemeris(bodies, lat, lon, d, _jd)` ignores its jd argument — d (days since J2000) is the only clock; passing jd where d is expected shifts the epoch ~2.45 million days (≈6,700 years) into the future.
- Time scale is UTC-as-UT1 throughout — no ΔT/leap-second/TDB handling in any entry above.
