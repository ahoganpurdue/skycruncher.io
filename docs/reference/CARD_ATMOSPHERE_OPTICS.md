# Atmosphere & Optics Physics — algorithm card (extinction, refraction, PSF-predictor lane)

This card covers everything the codebase models about light traveling through the atmosphere and through the optical system before it reaches the sensor: airmass and extinction, atmospheric refraction, seeing, diffraction, coma, and lens distortion/vignetting. Most of this lane is reported and predicted rather than corrected — it exists to give an honest physical account of what's degrading a given frame, not (yet) to fix it.

Symbols: h=apparent altitude (DEGREES), z=zenith distance (90−h), X=airmass, τ=zenith optical depth, Δm=extinction (mag), λ=wavelength, D=aperture diameter (mm), FL=focal length (mm), f#=focal ratio, δ=Dec, RA in **HOURS** unless stated, ″=arcsec, ′=arcmin, r=normalized radial coordinate.
Every entry below is VERIFIED against the code at the cited line; formulas transcribed from the running code, not idealized from the literature.

### 1. Kasten & Young (1989) airmass — plus the COMPETING plane-parallel sec z model in the PSF lane
Airmass measures how much atmosphere a line of sight passes through, driving extinction (entry 2) and seeing (entry 4). Two different, independently coded airmass formulas exist in this codebase and disagree with each other and with a third consumer near the horizon.
- **Formula (extinction lane)**: $X = 1 / (\sin(h_{rad}) + 0.50572\cdot(6.07995 + h)^{-1.6364})$. Caps: $h \le 0 \to X = 40$ (below-horizon cap, includes h=0 exactly); $h \ge 90 \to X = 1.0$.
- **Formula (PSF lane, competing)**: $X = 1 / \max(0.05, \cos z)$ — plane-parallel sec z with the cosine clamped at 0.05, i.e. an implicit cap of $X = 20$.
- **Code**: `src/engine/core/AtmosphericManager.ts:25-32` (`computeAirMass`; cap `:26`, formula `:31`); variant `src/engine/pipeline/m10_psf/psf_physics.ts:162-165` (`airmassFromAltitude`, self-labeled "APPROXIMATE below ~30°"). PSF consumer additionally nulls airmass when altitude ≤ 3° (`src/engine/pipeline/stages/psf_attribution.ts:355`).
- **Cite**: Kasten & Young (1989), "Revised optical air mass tables and approximation formula", Applied Optics 28(22), 4735.
- **Gotcha**: TWO airmass models coexist for the same sky with different saturation behavior (K&Y cap 40 vs sec-z cap 20 vs PSF-consumer null below 3° alt). Also a house unit trap baked into K&Y's own published form: the polynomial term $(6.07995 + h)^{-1.6364}$ takes h in **DEGREES** while $\sin$ takes RADIANS — a well-meaning "convert everything to radians" refactor silently breaks the formula.

### 2. Allen zenith Rayleigh optical depths + Δm = τ·X + zenith flux multipliers
Models how much each color channel dims with airmass due to Rayleigh scattering, and applies that dimming as a per-channel correction on the way from sky to sensor.
- **Constants**: $\tau_R = 0.044$ (comment: ~620 nm), $\tau_G = 0.098$ (~530 nm), $\tau_B = 0.235$ (~450 nm) — `AtmosphericManager.ts:17-19`, in-code attribution "Allen's, 4th ed."
- **Formula**: per-channel extinction $\Delta m = \tau\cdot X$ (`rayleighExtinction`, `:37-43`); restoration multipliers $m_c = 10^{0.4\Delta m_c}$ (`getZenithMultipliers`, `:49-56`).
- **Application**: `src/engine/pipeline/m5_coordinate_flatten/zenith.ts:276-292` (`applyZenithCorrection`) — the pixel is converted sRGB→CIE XYZ, then the RGB-keyed multipliers are applied on the TRISTIMULUS axes: $X \leftarrow X\cdot(m_R+m_G)/2$, $Y \leftarrow Y\cdot m_G$, $Z \leftarrow Z\cdot m_B$, then XYZ→sRGB back.
- **Cite**: values consistent with the Rayleigh-scattering optical depths in *Allen's Astrophysical Quantities*, 4th ed. (Cox, ed., 2000) as the in-code comment claims [VERIFY — reference volume, not checked against the printed table this pass].
- **Gotcha (documented drift, ×2)**: (a) the $\tau\to$mag factor **1.0857** ($= 2.5\log_{10}e$) is ABSENT from shipped code — grep of `src/` finds zero hits; the live form $\Delta m = \tau\cdot X$ runs a factor 1.0857 (~7.9%) BELOW the physically-correct $\Delta m = 1.0857\cdot\tau\cdot X$. Tracked as an unharvested fix in `docs/NEXT_MOVES.md:40` ("Rayleigh 1.0857 factor"). (b) the docstring at `zenith.ts:282` claims "Work in linear light (remove gamma) → correct → reapply gamma", but the correction is applied in CIE XYZ, not linear RGB — X is not the R primary (hence the $(m_R+m_G)/2$ hack); this is the same NEXT_MOVES line's "zenith linear-RGB" item. Cross-lane note: the green channel is keyed to ~530 nm here but $\lambda_g = 0.55\ \mu m$ in the diffraction lane (entry 5).

### 3. Bennett refraction + field differential refraction (revived predictor-only consumer)
Predicts how much the atmosphere bends starlight near the horizon, and how that bending varies across a wide field of view — reported for the PSF diagnostics only, never used to correct a solve.
- **Formula**: $R = 1 / \tan(h + 7.31/(h + 4.4))$ ARCMINUTES at standard pressure/temperature (in-code: 1010 hPa, 10 °C); h clamped ≥ 0.01° to dodge the horizon singularity; returned ×60 as ARCSECONDS. Differential: $\Delta R(h_1,h_2) = R(h_1) - R(h_2)$.
- **Code**: `src/engine/core/optics_manager.ts:290-298` (`calculateAtmosphericRefraction`), `:304-308` (`getDifferentialRefraction`). Wrapper: `src/engine/pipeline/m5_coordinate_flatten/differential_refraction_corrector.ts:23-41` (thin delegation). LIVE consumer: `src/engine/pipeline/stages/psf_attribution.ts:375-391` — gated on trusted clock AND real GPS (`gpsReal && clockReal`, `:347-349,376`), computes the plate stretch across the vertical field: altTop = min(89.9, h + halfFOV), altBot = max(0.1, h − halfFOV), halfFOV = (imageHeight/2)·pixelScale/3600.
- **Cite**: Bennett (1982), "The Calculation of Astronomical Refraction in Marine Navigation", Journal of Navigation 35(2), 255. The formula formerly quoted in the wrapper header was Sæmundsson's (now disclaimed there) — Sky & Telescope 72, 70 (1986) [VERIFY — hobbyist-magazine source, NOT the shipped formula; see Gotcha].
- **Gotcha (header since corrected — retained for lineage)**: the wrapper header was previously stale on two counts and has since been fixed. It now reads "STATUS: LIVE as an APPROXIMATE, clock+GPS-gated field-level PREDICTOR" (`differential_refraction_corrector.ts:11-21`) and explicitly disclaims the old "R = 1.02·cot(h_a + 10.3/(h_a + 5.11))" Sæmundsson form, using the true Bennett 7.31/4.4 form the executing code runs. (Historically the header claimed "UNWIRED / INERT — this class is not imported by any pipeline stage" — FALSE since the revival, `psf_attribution.ts:38` imports it and `:380` calls `computeDifferential` every gated run — and mislabeled the Sæmundsson form as Bennett; both corrected.) Still PREDICTOR ONLY: the result feeds the PSF report, never the solve chain (`:389` note); the chromatic single-star dispersion term is explicitly DEFERRED (achromatic model can't yield it).

### 4. Seeing airmass scaling θ = θ_zenith·(sec z)^0.6
Scales an assumed baseline seeing value with airmass, following the standard Kolmogorov-turbulence dependence — an approximation, not a measurement, since no real seeing monitor exists on these rigs.
- **Formula**: $\theta = \theta_{zenith} \cdot \max(1, X)^{0.6}$ arcsec, with $\theta_{zenith} =$ `DEFAULT_SEEING_ZENITH_ARCSEC` = 2.0″ — an ASSUMED amateur-site constant, never measured.
- **Code**: `src/engine/pipeline/m10_psf/psf_physics.ts:51` (constant), `:138-140` (`seeingArcsec`); consumer `psf_attribution.ts:358-373` (tier `APPROXIMATE`, falls back to X=1 when no observing geometry, and pushes the assumption into the report's `approximate[]` ledger).
- **Cite**: the 0.6 = 3/5 exponent follows from Kolmogorov turbulence, $r_0 \propto (\cos z)^{3/5}$ — Fried (1966), JOSA 56(10), 1372; the 2.0″ zenith constant has no source ('--', assumption).
- **Gotcha**: the airmass fed in is the PSF lane's plane-parallel sec z (entry 1 variant), NOT Kasten & Young — near the horizon the two lanes disagree about X and therefore about predicted seeing. $\max(1, X)$ silently floors nonsense sub-1 airmass instead of rejecting it.

### 5. Chromatic diffraction floor: Airy FWHM 1.028·λ/D + Rayleigh first null 1.22·λ/D
Sets the physically unavoidable lower bound on star size (FWHM) that diffraction alone imposes, given the aperture and per-channel wavelength — a floor the PSF measurement is checked against, never subtracted from it.
- **Formula**: $\theta_{FWHM} = 1.028\cdot(\lambda/D)\cdot(648000/\pi)''$ with $D = FL/f\#$ (mm) and λ in mm (input µm × $10^{-3}$); $\theta_{Rayleigh} = 1.22\cdot\lambda/D$ (reported for reference only). Per-channel $\lambda = \{r: 0.62, g: 0.55, b: 0.47\}\ \mu m$. Arcsec-native, grid-independent — the caller divides by pixel scale to reach science-grid px.
- **Code**: `src/engine/pipeline/m10_psf/psf_physics.ts:43,45,48` (constants `DIFFRACTION_FWHM_COEFF`, `RAYLEIGH_COEFF`, `LAMBDA_UM`), `:124-128` (`diffractionFwhmArcsec`), `:130-133` (`rayleighArcsec`); consumer `psf_attribution.ts:325-344` (tier `CALCULATED`, `limitedGreen` flag = measured minPx ≤ 1.3× green floor).
- **Cite**: Airy (1835), "On the Diffraction of an Object-glass with Circular Aperture", Trans. Camb. Phil. Soc. 5, 283; Rayleigh (1879), Phil. Mag. 8, 261 (resolution criterion). The 1.028 FWHM coefficient of the Airy core is textbook optics (Born & Wolf, *Principles of Optics*, §8.6) [VERIFY — textbook constant, not re-derived here].
- **Gotcha**: a FLOOR, never a subtraction — the consumer's note (`psf_attribution.ts:340`) states it explicitly; using it to deconvolve would be a category error. D = FL/f# inherits every upstream focal-length lie (the 50 mm factory-default EXIF trap, `optics_manager.ts:46-61`); when FL/f#/scale are missing the floor is honestly NOT MEASURED (`:343`), not defaulted.

### 6. Sidereal drift + det-sign-aware CD axis directions + parallactic angle + zenith image PA + drift kernel
Describes how a star trails on an untracked mount, which direction "up" and "north" point in a solved image, and how the sky's apparent rotation (parallactic angle) orients that trail — geometry that is reported, and could feed a future known-kernel deblur, but is never itself corrected.
- **Drift**: $trail = 15.041''/s \cdot \cos\delta \cdot t_{exposure}$ (`SIDEREAL_RATE_ARCSEC_PER_SEC` `psf_physics.ts:37`, `siderealTrailArcsec` `:76-78`) — constant stored truncated from $360°\cdot3600''/86164.0905\,s = 15.04107$.
- **Axis directions** (CD in deg/px): RA axis = $\operatorname{atan2}(-cd_{10}\cdot sgn,\ cd_{11}\cdot sgn)$, Dec axis = $\operatorname{atan2}(cd_{00}\cdot sgn,\ -cd_{01}\cdot sgn)$, $sgn = \operatorname{sign}(\det CD)$ — the det sign encodes parity and determines the full $[0,360)$ direction; it cancels under the $[0,180)$ line fold (`:83-103`). `synthesizeCd(scale, roll, parity)` builds an approximate CD when no fitted one exists, folding parity into the ξ-row sign (`:108-118`).
- **Geometry**: local GMST = $(280.46061837 + 360.98564736629\cdot(JD-2451545.0)) \bmod 360$, JD from Unix epoch (`targetGeometry` `:146-160`, RA input in HOURS, ×15 at `:152`); parallactic angle $q = \operatorname{atan2}(\sin H,\ \tan\phi\cos\delta - \sin\delta\cos H)$ (`:168-172`); zenith image-space PA rotates image North by $\pm q$ toward image East with the sense read from the CD handedness (`zenithPaImageDeg` `:181-190`).
- **Drift kernel**: exact uniform 1-D line ("top-hat along the trail") of `lengthPx` at `paDeg` — DESCRIBED only, never deconvolved (`driftPsfKernel` `:303-310`, follow-on seam for a known-kernel deblur).
- **Cite**: sidereal day 86164.0905 s per IAU-1982 (Aoki et al. 1982, A&A 105, 359); the linear GMST form and the parallactic-angle formula match Meeus, *Astronomical Algorithms*, 2nd ed., chs. 12 & 14 [VERIFY — practical textbook, not peer-reviewed; the GMST here drops Meeus's $T^2$ and $T^3$ terms].
- **Gotcha**: this is a SECOND, inline GMST implementation, independent of `TimeService` (whose IAU-1982 continuous form is the house convention) — it treats UTC as UT1 and omits the quadratic terms; fine for drift PAs, not for precision timing. Untracked-mount assumption: `siderealTrailArcsec` models a FIXED tripod; any tracking (even partial) makes the "exact" trail an upper bound.

### 7. Coma form fit — 1-parameter through-origin least squares e(r) = k·r
Fits how much a star's elongation (ellipticity) grows linearly with distance from the field center — the signature of coma aberration — and checks whether the elongation actually points radially outward before trusting the fit as coma rather than something else (drift, seeing, tilt).
- **Formula**: $k = \sum(r_i e_i) / \sum(r_i^2)$ over 3×3 field regions; $r$ = region distance from field center / half-diagonal (normalized [0,1], center region $r < 10^{-6}$ skipped — no radial axis). Consistency: median over regions of the line-angle separation between measured major-axis PA and the radial PA (`lineAngleSepDeg`, [0,90]); `patternConsistent` ⟺ $k > 0$ AND medianDev ≤ 30° (`radialTolDeg` default) AND $n \ge 4$ (`minRegions` default). Through-origin $R^2 = 1 - SS_{res}/SS_{tot}$ with $SS_{tot}$ about the MEAN.
- **Code**: `src/engine/pipeline/m10_psf/psf_physics.ts:227-284` (`fitComaCoefficient`; slope `:256`, $R^2$ `:258-272`, median `:274-275`, gate `:276`); consumer `psf_attribution.ts:399-418` (9-region grid, tier `FITTED`, coefficient never fabricated from EXIF).
- **Cite**: the coma FORM (transverse blur growing linearly with field height, flare oriented radially) is third-order Seidel aberration theory — Born & Wolf, *Principles of Optics*, ch. 5 [VERIFY — textbook]; the 1-parameter fit itself is bespoke ('--').
- **Gotcha**: the $R^2$ pairs a through-origin model with a mean-centered total sum of squares — statistically inconsistent (standard through-origin $R^2$ uses the uncentered SS) and can go NEGATIVE when the fit is worse than the mean; the median of an even-length deviation list takes the UPPER middle element (`devs[len>>1]`). The output field name `coeffPerPx` (and its "per px of field radius" doc, `psf_physics.ts:206`) is a MISNOMER — r is normalized to the half-diagonal, so k is ellipticity per unit NORMALIZED radius (i.e. at the field corner), not per pixel. The fitted ellipticity is the TOTAL measured field — drift/seeing/tilt contaminate k; only the radial-orientation gate separates coma from impostors.

### 8. SECOND Brown-Conrady implementation + vignetting fit + FOV + focal-length recovery
A separate, more complete lens-geometry engine used by the coordinate-flattening path: full radial-plus-tangential distortion (as opposed to CARD_ASTROMETRY_WCS.md's radial-only variant), a vignetting brightness model, and field-of-view/focal-length bookkeeping.
- **Forward (ideal→distorted)**: with normalized $(x_n,y_n) = ((x,y) - center)/r_{ref}$, $r^2 = x_n^2+y_n^2$: radial $= 1 + k_1 r^2 + k_2 r^4 + k_3 r^6$; $dx_{tang} = 2p_1 x_n y_n + p_2(r^2+2x_n^2)$; $dy_{tang} = p_1(r^2+2y_n^2) + 2p_2 x_n y_n$; out = center + $(x_n\cdot\text{radial} + d_{tang})\cdot r_{ref}$. $r_{ref}$ = profile.r_ref, else half-diagonal when width,height > 0, else 1.0.
- **Inverse (distorted→ideal)**: TRUE Newton-Raphson on the radial scalar, 10 iterations: $f = r_u(1+k_1r_u^2+k_2r_u^4+k_3r_u^6) - r_d$, $f' = 1+3k_1r_u^2+5k_2r_u^4+7k_3r_u^6$, early-out $|f| < 10^{-7}$; then a "simplified" tangential removal — the forward tangential terms are SUBTRACTED, evaluated on a HYBRID: distorted normalized (x,y) but $r^2 = r_u^2$ from the NR result (first-order approximation, code comment admits "p1, p2 are usually tiny").
- **Coefficient solver**: regress $y = (r_d/r_u) - 1$ on $\{r^2, r^4, r^6\}$ via 3×3 normal equations solved by Cramer's rule (`solve3x3`, determinant guard $10^{-15}$); needs ≥ 6 pairs; $p_1 = p_2 = 0$ pinned in the output profile.
- **Vignetting**: factor $= \sum_i coeffs_i\cdot r^{2i}$, clamped [0,1]; correction $= 1/\text{factor}$ gated factor > 0.01; profile fit = through-origin regression of (intensity − 1) on $r^2$ → coeffs [1.0, $k_1$], needs ≥ 5 points.
- **Also here**: FOV $= 2\arctan(d/(2\cdot FL))$ degrees (`getFov`); FL recovery $FL_{mm} = 206.265\cdot pitch_{\mu m}/scale_{''/px}$ (`recoverFocalLengthFromScale` — 206.265 = 206265 arcsec/rad ÷ 1000 for the µm→mm fold; post-solve, honest-or-absent).
- **Code**: `src/engine/core/optics_manager.ts:144-171` (`applyDistortion`), `:177-221` (`removeDistortion`; NR loop `:198-208`, tangential removal `:212-219`), `:227-264` (`solveDistortionPolynomial`), `:266-282` (`solve3x3`), `:316-320` (`getFov`), `:328-362` (vignette factor/correction), `:369-383` (`solveVignettingProfile`), `:131-138` (FL recovery). Live callers: `src/engine/core/SkyTransform.ts:66,86`; `src/engine/pipeline/m5_coordinate_flatten/coordinate_flattener.ts:131`; `src/engine/pipeline/m5_coordinate_flatten/generic_flattener.ts:44,58` (thin single-point delegation — live via `stages/detect.ts:74` and `orchestrator.ts:1115`; its BATCH path `flattenPoints` `:65-99` bypasses this class for WASM, see Gotcha).
- **Cite**: Brown (1966), "Decentering Distortion of Lenses", Photogrammetric Engineering 32(3), 444; Conrady (1919), "Decentered Lens-Systems", MNRAS 79, 384. Vignetting: the fit is a bespoke generic radial polynomial — NOT the $\cos^4$ law despite the interface's `cosPower` field ('--').
- **Gotcha**: DISTINCT from the already-carded radial-only `m2_hardware/lens_distortion.ts` ($k_1,k_2$, fixed-point inverse) — this one applies the FULL $k_1,k_2,k_3 + p_1,p_2$ model with a true Newton-Raphson radial inverse; and a THIRD Brown-Conrady implementation ships: a Rust WASM port of `removeDistortion` (`src/engine/wasm_compute/src/lib.rs:632`, `flatten_coordinates`), consumed by the batch path `generic_flattener.ts:65-99` (`flattenPoints`, live via `detect.ts:74` / `orchestrator.ts:1115` / `src/engine/tools/verify_pipeline.ts:245`). ## UNIT WARNINGS
- Altitude/h enters EVERY function on this card in DEGREES; radian conversion is internal. Kasten & Young deliberately mixes units (degrees inside the polynomial, radians inside sin) — do not "fix" it.
- TWO airmass conventions: extinction lane caps X=40 below horizon (`AtmosphericManager.ts:26`); PSF lane caps X=20 via the cos clamp (`psf_physics.ts:164`) and its consumer nulls X below 3° altitude.
- Refraction returns ARCSECONDS (`optics_manager.ts:297`, arcmin×60 internally) — Bennett's native published unit is arcminutes; a caller assuming arcmin is 60× wrong.
- Wavelength bookkeeping differs per lane: extinction τs are keyed to nm in comments (620/530/450), diffraction takes µm (0.62/0.55/0.47) — and the GREEN centers disagree (530 vs 550 nm).
- Distortion coefficients are only meaningful WITH their normalization: half-diagonal PIXELS in the flattener lane vs tangent-plane DEGREES ($r_{ref}\vee1.0$) in the SkyTransform lane.
- RA is HOURS at the `targetGeometry` boundary (×15 to degrees at `psf_physics.ts:152`); CD matrices in the PSF lane are DEGREES/px.
- Zenith flux restoration multiplies CIE XYZ tristimulus axes, not linear RGB channels — despite the RGB-keyed multiplier names.
